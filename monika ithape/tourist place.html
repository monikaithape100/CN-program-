Sindhudurg District

Tourist Places in Sindhudurg:

Sindhudurg Caves


Sindhudurg district is spread over an area of around 5,207 sq.kms.
The population of the District is 8,68,825 as per census of 2001.
The modern township of Sindhudurg Nagari is the headquarters of Sindhudurg district .

Sindhudurg Fort


Sindhudurg Fort is a historical fort that occupies an islet in the Arabian Sea, just off the coast of Maharashtra in Western India.
The fortress lies on the shore of Malvan town of Sindhudurg District in the Konkan region of Maharashtra, 450 kilometres south of Mumbai.
The fort is a great spot for history enthusiasts and offers panoramic views of the surrounding area.

Sindhudurg Temple


Located in Tuljapur, around 30 km from Osmanabad, this famous temple is dedicated to Goddess Tulja Bhavani, one of the most revered deities in Maharashtra.
It is a significant pilgrimage site and is visited by thousands of devotees, especially during Navratri.

Terna River


The river is not only a source of irrigation but also a peaceful spot where tourists can visit to relax, enjoy picnics, or take walks along the riverbanks.
