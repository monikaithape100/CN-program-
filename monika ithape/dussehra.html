why it is celebrated?

Dussehra is celebrated on the occasion of the victory of Lord Rama over Ravana. People used to enact Ram-Leela before Dussehra. Subsequently, on the day of Dussehra, people make the statues of Ravana and his brothers and burn them. This depicts the victory of good over evil, a big lesson to learn.

when it ic celebrated?

on the 10th day of the month of Ashvina (September–October), the seventh month of the Hindu calendar, with the appearance of the full moon, an event called the “bright fortnight” (shukla paksha).

what do you do on this day?

Dussehra, also known as Vijayadashami, is a festival which falls on the tenth day of the Hindu month of Ashvin, at the end of Navratri. The festival is celebrated across India and marks the end of Durga Puja. In some regions, Dussehra signifies Lord Rama’s victory over Ravana. The festival also marks the beginning of preparation for Diwali, which comes after 20 days. In India, one can witness grand Dussehra celebrations in every locality, which typically include fireworks, burning of Ravana’s effigies, immersion of goddess Durga’s idols, etc.
